<p><p>The Injectable Bone Substitute market is divided into natural and synthetic types. Natural bone substitutes derive from human or animal sources, providing excellent biocompatibility and promoting bone regeneration. They include materials like demineralized bone matrix or collagen. In contrast, synthetic substitutes are man-made and offer consistency in their composition, enabling tailored properties such as porosity and strength. Common synthetic options include calcium phosphates and bioactive glass, making them favorable for various orthopedic and dental applications due to their durability and functionality.</p></p>
