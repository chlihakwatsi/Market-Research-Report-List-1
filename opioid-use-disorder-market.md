<p><p>The Opioid Use Disorder market comprises three primary treatment types: buprenorphine, methadone, and naltrexone. Buprenorphine is a partial opioid agonist used for managing cravings and withdrawal symptoms, making it a popular choice for maintenance therapy. Methadone, a full agonist, is effective in reducing withdrawal symptoms and is often used in structured treatment programs. Naltrexone, an opioid antagonist, blocks the euphoric effects of opioids and is used primarily after detoxification to prevent relapse. Each treatment serves distinct patient needs in combating opioid addiction.</p></p>
