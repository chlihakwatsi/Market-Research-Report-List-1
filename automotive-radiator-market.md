<p><p>The automotive radiator market is primarily divided into two types: aluminum and copper radiators. Aluminum automotive radiators are lightweight, efficient, and resistant to corrosion, making them popular for modern vehicles. They offer better heat dissipation and are easier to manufacture in complex shapes. In contrast, copper automotive radiators, while heavier and less corrosion-resistant, provide superior thermal conductivity and durability, making them suitable for high-performance and vintage vehicles. Each type serves specific market needs based on vehicle application and performance requirements.</p></p>
